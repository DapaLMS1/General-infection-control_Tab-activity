Hand Hygiene

  • **Handwashing** is the most important infection control measure.
  • Wash hands after touching blood, body fluids, or contaminated items, and after removing gloves.
  • Use soap and water or alcohol-based hand rubs.
  • Keep fingernails short and clean. Avoid artificial nails, nail polish, and jewellery.
  • Dry hands with single-use paper towels.
  • **Wash hands:**
    • Before and after treating each patient.
    • Before starting and after finishing work.
    • After using the restroom or handling contaminated items.

Hand Care

  • Cover cuts and abrasions with waterproof dressings.
  • Use non-oil-based moisturisers.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

  • Provide and use appropriate **PPE**:
    • Gloves (appropriate type for the procedure).
    • Eye protection (goggles or face shields).
    • Masks.
    • Fluid-resistant gowns or aprons.
  • Use appropriate footwear.
  • Change gloves between patients and when damaged.
  • Never reuse disposable PPE.
  • **Latex Allergy**:
    • Be aware of latex allergy symptoms.
    • Provide latex-free alternatives.
